What is Stress?

Our bodies' reaction to pressure is stress. Stress can be caused by a variety of conditions or life events. When we encounter something new, unexpected, or that threatens our sense of self, or when we believe we have little control over a situation, it is frequently activated.

Symptoms of Physical Stress


blood pressure and pulse rise

breathing speeds up

digestive system slows down

immune activity decreases

muscles become more tense

sleepiness decreases due to a heightened state of alertness

Types of Stress


acute

chronic

Causes of Stress

Stress is a normal and necessary element of life to some extent. Despite the fact that stress is something that everyone goes through, what causes it might vary from person to person. A significant traffic delay, for example, may make one person irritated and stressed, while another may crank up their music and consider it a minor annoyance. A dispute with a buddy might linger for the rest of the day for one individual.

Stress Hormones

Cortisol, the principal stress hormone, raises blood sugar levels (glucose), improves glucose utilisation in the brain, and increases the availability of chemicals that repair cells. Cortisol also suppresses functions that aren't necessary or beneficial in a fight-or-flight situation.

Real Meaning of Stress

A sensation of emotional or bodily strain is referred to as stress. Any incident or idea that makes you annoyed, furious, or anxious might trigger it. Your body's response to a challenge or demand is called stress.
